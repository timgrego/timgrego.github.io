I’m a final-year BSc Computer Science student at the University of London (distance). Alongside my degree, I work as a research assistant with an associate professor and professor at Kyiv National University, where I’m developing methods around Lotka–Volterra dynamical systems. My current work focuses on detecting and characterising qualitative regime changes: I’ve built a [classifier](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/14hyrvOq-P15kOHfaZ-jK-YE5_oUGTOGy?usp=sharing) to identify where bifurcations occur, a [data sampler](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1_8-MxDX5rhN03MLnKzW6xNJGtExdcETp?usp=sharing) and I’m formalising discrete definitions of bifurcations to make these transitions easier to study computationally.

Looking ahead, I plan to focus my research on meta-level control in autonomous agents, extending my current interest in qualitative regime change in dynamical systems to questions of adaptation in agent–environment systems. My goal is to characterise the configurations under which agents require self-models to maintain bounded optimality in embedded settings, particularly under resource constraints, perturbations, distribution shift, and multi-agent interaction. I am particularly interested in the role self-models play in supporting reliable policy adaptation and improvement under such constraints. More broadly, I aim to connect foundational questions about agency with practical autonomy through a combination of formal modelling, information-theoretic objectives, and reproducible implementations in model-based reinforcement learning.
