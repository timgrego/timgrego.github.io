I’m a final-year BSc Computer Science student at the University of London (distance). Alongside my degree, I work as a research assistant with an associate professor and professor at Kyiv National University, where I’m developing methods around Lotka–Volterra dynamical systems. My current work focuses on detecting and characterising qualitative regime changes: I’ve built a [classifier](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/14hyrvOq-P15kOHfaZ-jK-YE5_oUGTOGy?usp=sharing) to identify where bifurcations occur, a [parameter sampler](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1_8-MxDX5rhN03MLnKzW6xNJGtExdcETp?usp=sharing) and I’m formalising discrete definitions of bifurcations to make these transitions easier to study computationally.

Looking ahead, I plan to focus my research on self-modelling in autonomous agents. My goal is to give a clean mathematical definition of a self-model, treating it as an internal predictive simulator of an agent’s own state, constraints, and action-generation process, and to study how self-models can be used for reliable policy improvement in embedded settings (where agents are resource-bounded, can be perturbed, and must remain robust under distribution shift). I’m especially interested in connecting foundations-of-agency questions to practical autonomy by combining formal modelling, information-theoretic objectives, and reproducible implementations in model-based reinforcement learning.
